BOLANGIR

1. Bhima Dunguri, located in the Balangir district of Odisha, stands out as a significant tourist destination owing to its captivating natural beauty and the presence of ancient caves scattered across the hilly landscape. This picturesque area is enveloped by evergreen forests, enhancing its allure for visitors seeking a serene and scenic retreat. One of the notable features of Bhima Dunguri is the cave situated atop Bhim Pahad, approximately 28 km from Bolangir town. This cave holds cultural and religious significance as it is dedicated to the worship of Lord Parshuram and Shiva. The site offers a unique blend of natural splendor and spiritual resonance. The charm of Bhima Dunguri is particularly pronounced during the spring season when the panoramic views of the surrounding landscape become truly unparalleled. The seasonal beauty of the area adds an extra layer of enchantment, making it a magnet for a diverse range of tourists.

2. The Dhabaleswar Temple, nestled at the foot of Kumuda Pahada Mountain in the Balangir district of Odisha, emerges as a captivating tourist destination. Located a mere 0.5 km from Titilagarh town, this temple is ensconced in natural splendor, set amidst hilly terrain. The main attraction lies in the expansive cave, measuring 80 feet by 40 feet, where Lord Dhabaleswar is revered. Three additional caves in close proximity add to the mystical ambiance of the site. Despite its modest size, the Dhabaleswar Temple holds significant religious importance, drawing visitors from far and wide. Lord Dhabaleswar's perceived benevolence and mercy make this temple a revered destination, attracting a substantial number of devotees, especially during the auspicious months of Shravana and Shivaratri. The site gains additional cultural richness with the presence of the Lord Shree Ram Temple in its vicinity. The landscape surrounding the hill is adorned with a small reservoir at its summit, contributing to the overall aesthetic appeal.

3. Gaikhai Dam , popularly known as M.I.P., is a picturesque destination located 30 km from Bolangir town. Nestled amidst green-capped hills on three sides, it offers a stunning scenic beauty that attracts tourists and visitors alike. The dam serves as a small irrigation project, primarily built for farming and year-round water harvesting. The tranquil waters and the natural surroundings make Gaikhai Dam an ideal spot for picnics, drawing people who seek to enjoy the serene environment and breathtaking views. The location is particularly appealing during the winter months, offering a cool breeze and a refreshing atmosphere. Gaikhai Dam, or M.I.P., stands out as a popular destination for weekend getaways, providing a perfect setting for group camping and attracting picnickers throughout the year. In essence, it is a haven for those seeking natural beauty and a peaceful retreat in the vicinity of Bolangir town.

4. Gandhamardan Hills , situated between Balangir and Bargarh districts in Odisha, India, is a region of immense ecological, cultural, and historical significance. Famous for its abundance of medicinal plants, the hills house a Bauxite reserve that was once under consideration for exploration through a private venture sanctioned by the state government. According to Hindu mythology, Lord Hanuman carried the Gandhamardan hill on his shoulders from the Himalayas to save the life of Lakshman during the Treta Yug. The hill is believed to be a portion that dropped during Hanuman's journey. The Nrusinghanath Temple is located on the northern slope, and the Harishankar Temple is on the southern slope of Gandhamardan, adding to the cultural richness of the area. During the Mahabharat period, Sage Drona resided on Gandhamardan, imparting the art of weaponry to selected pupils. Scholars speculate that this mountain might have been the site of Nagarjuna's monastery, a reference made by Hiuen-Tsiang in his chronicles.

5. Sri Sri Harisankar Devasthana is a significant temple situated on the slopes of Gandhamardhan hills in the Bolangir District of Odisha, India. Renowned for its natural beauty and association with Hindu deities Vishnu and Shiva, the temple offers a serene atmosphere with a stream flowing on a granite bed. The region between Harisankar and the Nrusinghanath temple has ancient Buddhist ruins, believed to be remnants of the Parimalgiri University. The temple's history dates back to the 14th century when the deity of Harisankar was discovered by a Chauhan dynasty king. Construction of the temple was commissioned by Queen Durlabha Devi of Maharaja Vaijjal Dev Chauhan. The area boasts a dancing Ganesha image dating back to the early 12th century. Harisankar is located in the Balangir district, with the nearest railway station being Harishankar Road, 32 km away. A stream with cascades, a deer park, and a variety of flora and fauna enhance the picturesque landscape.

7. Ranipur Jharial, located 104 km southwest of Balangir town in Odisha, India, stands atop a rocky hill and is home to the shrine dedicated to the presiding deity of 64 Yoginis. Known as "Soma Tirth" in scriptures, this site encompasses a unique blend of religious faiths, including Saivism, Buddhism, Vaisnavism, and Tantrism. The primary attraction is the circular, open-vault structure where the 64 Yoginis are worshipped, making it one of the four such shrines in India. The temple dedicated to Lord Someswar Siva is particularly notable among the approximately 50 temples in the area. Notably, the majestic brick temple known as Indralath is considered the highest brick temple in Odisha. The surviving central shrine within the Chausath Yogini Temple houses an image of dancing Shiva, with all Yogini images uniquely depicted in a dancing posture. Built in the 9th or 10th century, the Chausath Yogini Temple is circular, hypaethral, and made of sandstone, spanning approximately 50 feet in diameter.

8. Turekela, situated 98 km away from Balangir in Odisha, India, is a captivating destination known for its Turekela Wildlife Sanctuary, offering a perfect setting for group adventurism. The sanctuary is renowned for its vibrant and diverse wildlife, including tigers, deer, bears, foxes, elephants, various species of birds, monkeys, and wolves. The lush green and dense forests create an enchanting backdrop, providing an ideal environment for group camping and wildlife enthusiasts. The sanctuary's natural beauty, characterized by hills and dense forests, becomes particularly alluring during the winter season, attracting numerous tourists, including school and college students, weekend tourists, and those seeking leisure, to celebrate New Year in the lap of nature. The chirping birds perched on trees add to the thrill of the overall wildlife viewing experience. Turekela, a village in the Balangir district, is located in the Turekela Tehsil and has a subdistrict population of 73,717.
